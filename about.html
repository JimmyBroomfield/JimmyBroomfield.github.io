About Me

I am a PhD candidate in mathematics at the University of Minnesota. Before starting my program at Minnesota, I earned a Bachelor's degree in math, physics, and chemistry from the University of South Dakota. While there, I researched noble gas scintillation for dark matter detection under the direction of Dr. Dongming Kim and Dr. Yongchen Sun. After graduating I took a year off from my studies and held positions as a laboratory technician and as an adjunct instructor. The following year I entered a Master's program in mathematics at Minnesota State University. While at Minnesota State, I became interested in differential geometry, analysis, and partial differential equations. My Master's thesis was titled Results on the Pompieu Problem for Curves in R2, and as of now, I am still deciding on a topic of research for my PhD.

Hobbies

Rock Climbing

Rock climbing has become a major passion of mine. I do both top rope and lead climbing, and I also have enough gear to do outdoor climbing and rappeling.

Cooking

Cooking is another hobby of mine, and I have five years of cooking experience. My favorite styles of food to cook are Italian and Mexican.

Fitness

I enjoy many fitness activities for their health benefits and the fun I have doing them. I particularly like weight lifting, martial arts, and biking.

Interests Outside of Math

Hobbies help to enrich our lives and give us an outlet for destressing. For me cooking, climbing, and fitness have become my primary hobbies. Each activity has its own set of benefits and challenges, and as much as I love mathematics, it is important to me to have these hobbies. By breaking up study and research with other activities, I can return to my work feeling refreshed.
